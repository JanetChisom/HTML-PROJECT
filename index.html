SIMPLE YAM PORRIDGE RECIPE

Yam porridge, also known as asaro, is a classic Nigerian comfort food. It’s soft, rich, and slightly oily from palm oil, with a thick texture created by mashed yam.
It’s simple, filling, and perfect for any day you want something warm and satisfying.

Time

  • Prep time: 10–15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 20–25 minutes
  • Total time: 35–40 minutes

Serving

  • Serves 2–3 people.
  • Serve hot 🔥

Difficulty

  • Easy (Beginner-friendly)

Ingredients

  • Yam (½ tuber)
  • Palm oil
  • 2–3 fresh tomatoes (or blended)
  • 1 small onion
  • Pepper (to taste)
  • Seasoning cube + salt
  • Crayfish (optional)
  • Fish, meat, or egg (optional)
  • Water

Instruction

  1. Peel and cut yam into small cubes
  2. Rinse well
  3. Put yam in a pot
  4. Add water (just enough to cover it)
  5. Add onions, tomatoes, pepper, and a little salt
  6. Cook for 10–15 minutes
  7. Add palm oil, crayfish, and seasoning
  8. Add fish or meat if using
  9. When yam is soft, mash some pieces with a spoon
  10. Leave some chunks for texture
  11. Let it cook for another 5–10 minutes until thick
  12. Taste and adjust seasoning

Tips

  • Don’t add too much water—it should be thick, not watery
  • Stir often so it doesn’t burn
  • Palm oil gives it that authentic Nigerian taste
  • add scent leaves or spinach at the end for extra flavor

Note: Always use fresh ingredients for best taste.
